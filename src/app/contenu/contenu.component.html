To progress in running In running, progress does not necessarily mean quantity. Certainly it is necessary to carry out a certain volume of work, kilometers and sessions to progress in Running and Running, but it is also necessary that the energy spent by the runner in training is done wisely. Otherwise, beware of fatal errors which block many runners in their progress and which, in the worst cases, send them straight towards injury. Having a training plan truly adapted to your abilities is the first basic principle to respect. This training plan must respect a very precise structure, allow you to use all the physiological zones necessary to improve your abilities, and integrate all the principles specific to the target distance. Knowing these basic principles, and understanding how they are integrated and linked into your 10 km, half-marathon, marathon and trail training, is an additional guarantee of your success in running.
